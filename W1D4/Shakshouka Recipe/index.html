Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp of Extra virgin olive oil
  • Vegetables: 1 large chopped onion, 1 to 2 green bell peppers, and 2 minced garlic cloves.
  • Spices: coriander, cumin, paprika. If you like spicy shakshouka, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or catenne pepper.
  • Tomatoes: 6 fresh medium-sized tomatoes and about ½ cup of tomato sauce. It helps if your fresh tomatoes are soft and almost overripe.
    If tomatoes are not in season, you can replace them with 1 28-ounce can of whole tomatoes or 6 cups canned chopped tomatoes with their juices.
    If you use the canned tomato alternative, you can omit the tomato sauce.
  • Eggs: 6 large raw eggs
  • Garnish: A handful of fresh chopped parsley and mint just before serving adds freshness and a pop of color.
    And if you love your shakshuka with feta, then add a sprinkle of creamy feta cheese just before serving.

Wholesome Shakshouka Recipe

Description

Shakshouka is a traditional North African and Middle Eastern dish that consists of eggs poached in a flavorful tomato sauce. To make shakshouka, start by sautéing onions and bell peppers in a pan with olive oil.
Then add crushed tomatoes, spices such as cumin and paprika, and simmer for a few minutes. Next, create wells in the sauce and crack eggs into them. Cover the pan and cook until the eggs are set to your desired level of doneness.
Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with bread for dipping into the sauce. This dish can also be made with additional ingredients such as feta cheese, sausage, and various vegetables.

Directions

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil.
  2. Add the chopped onion and green bell peppers to the skillet and sauté for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened.
  3. Add minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of ground coriander, 1 teaspoon of ground cumin, and 1 teaspoon of paprika. Cook for 1-2 minutes more, or until fragrant.
  4. Add 6 fresh medium-sized tomatoes and about ½ cup of tomato sauce to the skillet. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the tomatoes have broken down and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Use a spoon to make 6 wells in the sauce. Carefully crack an egg into each well.
  6. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
  7. Remove the skillet from heat and garnish with fresh parsley.
  8. Serve with bread for dipping into the sauce.

    9. Enjoy your shakshouka!
